# Draft Message Workflow

Execute these steps IN ORDER. Complete each step fully and show the required OUTPUT before moving to the next step.

---

## Step 1: Understand the Request

Ask:

> **Who's this message for and what's the goal?**
> - Recipient: (Person, team, or channel)
> - Medium: (Slack, email, Loom script, etc.)
> - Goal: (What do you want them to do/know/feel?)
> - Key points: (What must be included?)
> - Tone: (Casual, formal, direct, warm?)

**STOP and wait for answers before proceeding.**

## Voice Guide

When drafting, aim for:

**Good writing style:**
- Starts with the point, not the preamble
- Uses "I" and "we" naturally
- Short paragraphs, often 1-2 sentences
- Direct but not cold
- Shows appreciation genuinely, not performatively
- Asks clear questions when needed
- Ends with clear next step or CTA

**Avoid:**
- Corporate speak ("per my last email", "circle back", "synergize")
- Overly formal openings ("I hope this email finds you well")
- Passive voice when active is clearer
- Unnecessary hedging ("I was just wondering if maybe...")
